# DASH Diet

**Clinical Focus:** Low sodium, high potassium, rich in fruits and vegetables.  
**Macro Targets:** Carbs 52% | Protein 18% | Fat 30%

## Core Principles

- Mechanically designed to lower blood pressure through micronutrient balance
- Restrict sodium while increasing potassium, magnesium, and calcium
- Emphasize whole foods over processed options
- Balance macronutrients for heart health

## Implementation Guidelines

### Sodium Restrictions

```
Standard DASH: <2,300mg sodium per day
Therapeutic DASH: <1,500mg sodium per day
Avoid: Processed foods, canned soups, deli meats, restaurant meals
Read labels: Look for "low sodium" or "no salt added"
Seasoning: Use herbs, spices, lemon, vinegar instead of salt
```

### Potassium Boost

```
Daily Target: 4,700mg potassium
Top Sources:
  - Bananas (422mg each)
  - Sweet potatoes (542mg each)
  - Spinach (839mg per cup cooked)
  - White beans (1,189mg per cup)
  - Yogurt (573mg per cup)
```

### Calcium Sources

```
Low-fat dairy: Milk, yogurt, cheese (2-3 servings daily)
Fortified alternatives: Almond milk, soy milk
Leafy greens: Kale, collard greens
Canned fish with bones: Sardines, salmon
```

### Meal Structure Example

```
Breakfast: Oatmeal + banana + low-fat milk
Lunch: Grilled chicken + large mixed salad + olive oil
Dinner: Baked salmon + quinoa + steamed broccoli
Snacks: Unsalted nuts, fresh fruit, low-fat yogurt
```

## Key Nutrients to Emphasize

- **Potassium:** 4,700mg daily (lowers blood pressure)
- **Magnesium:** 500mg daily (relaxes blood vessels)
- **Calcium:** 1,200mg daily (vascular health)
- **Fiber:** 30g+ daily (cardiovascular protection)

## When to Use

- Hypertension management (primary use)
- Prehypertension prevention
- Cardiovascular disease prevention
- Stroke risk reduction
- Diabetes management (diabetic friendly)
